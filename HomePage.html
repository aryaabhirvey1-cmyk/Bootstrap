Social Change

The Tipping point I got wrong

The Tipping Point explains how small actions or ideas can suddenly create a big social change. It says that when enough people adopt a behavior, it reaches a “tipping point” and spreads rapidly. Sometimes, we think large change needs big efforts, but even small, consistent actions can trigger it. A few influential people or small events can push a society toward a new direction. The book shows how trends, habits, and movements often grow quietly before exploding. It teaches that even if we get something wrong at first, small corrections can still create major impact later.
